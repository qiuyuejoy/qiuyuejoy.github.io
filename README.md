<h2>About Me</h2>

<p>
I am a Computer Science Ph.D. student in Human-Computer Interaction at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, advised by 
<a href="https://people.cs.umass.edu/~rkarkar/">Professor Ravi Karkar</a>. 
My research lies at the intersection of human-centered AI, social computing, and positive computing.
</p>

<p>
With a cross-disciplinary background in business and law, I study how technologies can support well-being across personal, interpersonal, and organizational contexts. My work explores how AI systems can support emotional regulation, meaningful interaction, and decision-making for collective well-being.

## Research Projects

- **AI for Workplace Communication Skills** — *Apr 2026 – Present*  
  **Advisor:** Ravi Karkar  
  Prototyping an AI system for workplace communication that delivers personalized, context-aware feedback by modeling relationship dynamics, communication intent, and user-defined goals while preserving user agency and authenticity.

- **Lived Informatics of Positive Computing** — *Jan 2026 – May 2026*  
  **Advisors:** Ravi Karkar, Koustuv Saha, DongWhi Yoo, Violeta J. Rodriguez  
  Led a qualitative study examining gratitude as an evolving practice and developed a multilevel framework modeling engagement dynamics and sustainability mechanisms to inform the design of long-term well-being technologies.

- **Knowledge Gatekeeping in Media Ecosystems** — *Sep 2025 – Jan 2026*  
  **Advisors:** Ravi Karkar, Ethan Zuckerman  
  Led a large-scale computational analysis of media coverage and academic research dissemination, developing an LLM-driven pipeline to examine how institutional gatekeeping shapes knowledge diffusion.

- **Repair Rupture in Human-AI Chatbots** — *Nov 2025 – Apr 2026*  
  **Advisors:** Ravi Karkar, Koustuv Saha, DongWhi Yoo, Violeta J. Rodriguez  
  Co-investigated rupture recognition in AI chatbots and evaluated its alignment with clinician assessments. Designed clinically informed repair mechanisms to support safe and effective human-AI collaboration in mental health contexts.

- **AI on My Shoulder** — *Apr 2024 – Sep 2024*  
  **Advisors:** Vedant Das Swain, Varun Mishra  
  Designed and evaluated an LLM-based empathetic coworker system to support emotional labor in frontline workplace settings, examining how AI augmentation influences individual and workplace well-being.

- **Baidu Web Design and Development & “Doorstep” Online-to-Offline Business** — *2014 – 2017*
